Water Pollution in Indigenous Communities

What is the root cause of the unclean water in indigenous communities?

Poor drinking water quality on reserve is also related to poor quality source water. A lack of funding for infrastructure, training, and maintenance. Inconsistent disinfection, as well as distribution and operational issues. High risks of contamination due to rural and remote locations of reserves. Indigenous communities are vulnerable to water contamination due to a few factors like marginalization, contamination, remote areas and maintenance of systems. Governments only give limited funding to combat water contamination.

Where does water pollution occur?

Poor drinking water quality often occurs in the Prairie provinces like Alberta, Saskatchewan and Manitoba, as well as southern Ontario, and Quebec. This is where most of the agricultural and industrial activities occur. These areas, also happen to be where some indigenous communities live. As a result of agriculture, and industrial activites, chemical elements like nitrogen, and phosphorus are released into the environment.

How does the lack of clean water affect indigenous peoples?

A lack of clean water restricts indigenous access to traditional food systems from historically significant bodies of water and also increases the financial burden on native peoples. A single drinking water advisory can mean as many at 5,000 people lack access to safe, clean drinking water. 73 per cent of First Nations' water systems are at high or medium risk of contamination. Lack of access to clean, safe drinking water in First Nations must be fixed for good.
